This program was written to calculate the Cement, Sand, and Granite required in a given cubic meter of concrete.
This program is written specifically for Metric users. It requires the user to input the dimension and the mix ratio of the concrete.

How it works

A user needs to enter the length, width, & depth of the concrete. The user must also specify the mix ratio of the materials. All of which must be purely numerical.

Constants Used

Depending on the region, bags of cements may vary in kilograms. Also, density of sand and granite may vary from 1450 to 2080 Kg/m3 depending on lots of factors.

This program however has employed the following constant in its adjudication:

  • Bag of cement = 50kg
  • Density of Cement = 1440kg/m3
  • Density of Sand = 1450kg/m3
  • Density of Granite = 1550kg/m3

Breakdown

To use this program, a person must understand two (2) concrete concepts:
1. Dimension. 2. Mix ratio.

Dimension

Physical objects have dimensions. They could be in linear (length), square (length x breadth) or in cubic (length x breadth x height, or length x breadth x depth, or length x width x depth). Concrete is measured in cubic. A user of this program must know these details about the concrete they wish to calculate.

Mix ratio

This determines the grade (strength) of the concrete. It is the proportion of the cement, sand, and granite/gravel/rocks in the concrete.

This proportion can be measured in head-pans, batch boxes, buckets, etc. The smaller the mix ratio, the higher the Strength of the concrete. That is a mix ratio of 1:2:4 is greater than 1:3:6, and is in turn greater than 1:4:8 in strength.

Mix ratio 1:4:8 means 1 head-pan of cement, 4 head-pans of sand & 8 head-pans of granite in that order. You can read more on mix ratio and the appropriate projects to use them.
